# Final Abstract Draft: Claim-Safe

Status: POST-8 submission package
Claim ceiling: `operational_utility_only/no_claim_upgrade`

We study dispatch-time evidence selection for context projection under a
formal V-information motivation and deployed live-API constraints. The paper
does not claim V-information verification. Instead, it presents a
fail-closed, replayable, claim-gated operational audit framework for selecting
and evaluating evidence under token budgets. The framework separates formal
objectives, backend capability, operational replay, weak-evidence diagnostics,
and artifact replayability. Because the live API exposes generated-token chat
logprobs rather than fixed-target teacher-forced NLL, we do not relabel those
logprobs as teacher-forced NLL and do not claim a metric bridge. The empirical
package is therefore intentionally scoped: it reports operational evaluation
under named conditions, replayable artifact interfaces, sufficiency and
abstention diagnostics, reprojection witnesses, and model-adjudicated
candidate evidence only. The submission does not claim measurement validation,
calibrated proxy support, V-information proxy support, paper evidence,
selector superiority, Route 5 unlock, or Route 8 unlock.
